            <h1 class="project-title">Digital Illustration & Published Poetry Book: PANTONE 3537 UP</h1>
            
            <p class="project-summary">
                A self-published book of LGBTQ+ poetry featuring original digital illustrations, combining classical Greek sculptural influences with contemporary personal narrative. Written, illustrated, and published under my authorship as Sean August Horvath, the book has maintained consistent sales since its 2018 publication, including recent purchases in 2024.
            </p>

        <h2 class="section-header">Project Scope</h2>
        <ul class="feature-list">
            <li>Complete book conceptualization and execution</li>
            <li>Original poetry writing and editing</li>
            <li>Digital illustration creation for every poem</li>
            <li>Book design and layout</li>
            <li>Self-publishing process management</li>
            <li>Post-publication marketing and distribution</li>
        </ul>

        <img class="full-width-image" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/seanivore/portfolio/refs/heads/master/assets/entries/pantone-3537-book-published-illustrated/page-from-poetry-published-book-02.webp" alt="Poetry book spread showing illustration and text">

        <p>The illustrations draw inspiration from classical Greek sculpture while incorporating contemporary elements from personal photography and memories. This fusion creates a unique visual narrative that complements the poetry's themes of love, growth, and self-discovery.</p>

        <img class="full-width-image" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/seanivore/portfolio/refs/heads/master/assets/entries/pantone-3537-book-published-illustrated/page-from-poetry-published-book-03.webp" alt="Poetry book spread showing illustration and text">

        <h2 class="section-header">Publication Impact</h2>
        <ul class="feature-list">
            <li>Featured review on BookLife (Publishers Weekly platform)</li>
            <li>Consistent sales through Barnes & Noble and local consignment</li>
            <li>Particularly resonant with young LGBTQ+ readers</li>
            <li>Successfully pitched to multiple literary blogs and media outlets</li>
        </ul>

        <p>Read the full <a href="https://booklife.com/project/pantone-3537-up-36219" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">BookLife Review</a></p>

        <h2 class="section-header">Personal Note</h2>
        <p>Writing this book fulfilled dual purposes: personal catharsis and creating the kind of resource I wished existed during my own coming-of-age journey. While the poems capture the raw emotions of early-20s love, the project's true value lies in its contribution to LGBTQ+ youth literature and its demonstration of how personal experience can be transformed into professional creative work.</p>
